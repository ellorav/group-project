Health benefits of drinking water

Hydrating your body increases its power to work to its full capacity. Not only will you avoid feeling tired, but you'll never have an excuse to be lazy again.
Water can increase satiety and boost your metabolic rate. The timing is important too, and drinking water half an hour before meals is the most effective. It can make you feel more full so that you eat fewer calories.
Drinking water has been found to increase blood flow to the skin and may help improve skin density. By hydrating your skin, water also reduces toxin-caused blemishes - it works better than any moisturiser!
Higher fluid intake increases the volume of urine passing through the kidneys, which dilutes the concentration of minerals, so they are less likely to crystalise and form clumps.
Physical performance can suffer due to dehydration. Proper hydration helps keep joints lubricated and muscles more elastic so joint pain is also less likely.
Increasing fluid intake is often recommended to fight constipation, as water encourages regular bowel movements.
Alcohol consumption leads to dehydration as alcohol is a diuretic (makes you lose more water than you take in). The more water you drink, the more fluids you replace and the better you will feel. While nursing a bottle of water the next morning will certainly help your hangover, this method is best used preventatively.
Dehydration can affect your mood and make you feel sad, grumpy and confused. Water stimulates the flow of nutrients and hormones that release feel-good endorphines.
Studies show that even mild dehydration can impair many aspects of brain function. Water is required by every cell in the body and important for all body functions. It helps transport oxygen and nutrients critical to the brain for optimal function.
